**Narrator:** The terracotta giant. Alfonso Lombardi's Saint Bartholomew. If you enter the Sanctuary of the Madonna della Pioggia, your eyes will be immediately captured by the high altar. But there is a protagonist, evicted by history, waiting for you on the upper floor.

It is Alfonso Lombardi's Saint Bartholomew, a monumental statue that, for centuries, was the beating heart and the very symbol of this church. Imagine the scene: it is 1729. A sudden collapse severely damages the structure. During the reconstruction of 1732, Bologna decides to give the place of honor to the miraculous cult of the Madonna della Pioggia.

Thus, poor Saint Bartholomew, who until then had reigned supreme over the high altar, has to yield his throne and is moved to the oratory, where we still find him today. But do not be fooled by the relocation: his power remains intact. We are talking about a masterpiece from the early sixteenth century. Alfonso Lombardi, one of the greatest sculptors active in Bologna, shaped it in polychrome terracotta.

He is a giant of 2 meters and 25 centimeters, a commanding physical presence. Look at the modeling: it is muscular, pained, dramatic. As the saint was martyred by flaying, Lombardi infused the clay with a raw and truthful expressiveness that leaves you breathless. It is the force of the earth becoming flesh and spirit.

Finding this statue within the intimacy of the oratory is an almost magical experience: it is a face-to-face encounter with the Bolognese Renaissance. The next time you visit the Sanctuary, do not stop at the nave; go up the stairs, look for Saint Bartholomew. You will discover that, even far from the high altar, this terracotta giant has continued to tell his story of devotion and beauty for nearly 500 years.
