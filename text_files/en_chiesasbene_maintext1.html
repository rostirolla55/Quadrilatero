The Church of San Benedetto in Bologna

Line spacing: 1.15

The Church of San Benedetto in Bologna, located on Via Indipendenza, has a long and complex history:

  • Origins: Its origins date back to the 13th century (some documents mention it as a parish as early as 1202). It was initially located just outside Bologna's second ring of city walls.

  • 17th-Century Reconstruction: It was completely rebuilt in 1606 based on a design by Giovanni Battista Ballerini. During this period, it was officiated by the Minim Friars of St. Francis of Paola (known as the "Paolotti"), to whom it had been assigned in 1529.

  • Original Orientation: Until the end of the 19th century, the church faced Via Galliera, where the ancient facade (later remodeled in 1932) flanked by the bell tower is still visible today.
