• Interior: Despite the external transformations, the interior preserves important works by leading artists of the Bolognese Manierism and Baroque periods, including paintings by Giacomo Cavedoni, Alessandro Tiarini, and Lucio Massari.

In summary, the current appearance of the church is the result of its 17th-century reconstruction and the radical reorientation at the end of the 19th century, when it was "turned around" to face the modern Via Indipendenza.

Curiosities and Anecdotes

In addition to its architectural significance, the church holds unique stories. The 1892 transformation represented a true urban "flip": the original entrance on Via Galliera became the back wall of the altar, while the apse was demolished to make room for the new facade on Via Indipendenza. Furthermore, the complex was historically managed by the Minim Friars (called "Paolotti"), famous in Bologna for their strict dietary rule that forbade meat and animal products, forcing them to invent ingenious "lean" recipes in the heart of the ultimate gastronomic city. Despite the severe bombings that hit the area in 1943, the precious internal paintings were miraculously saved.
