"Close your eyes for a moment and listen. Beneath your feet, in the underground between via Azzo Gardino and Largo Caduti del Lavoro, beats the secret and invisible heart of Bologna. You are standing exactly above the ancient Port of the city, where the Salara, the old salt warehouse, once stood, and where the Cavaticcio Canal takes a spectacular plunge.

Here, protected by the darkness of the earth, lies an extraordinary record: one of the very few hydroelectric plants in the world perfectly functioning right in the historic center of a city of art. Inaugurated in nineteen-ninety-four and recently renovated with a modern technological revamping intervention in twenty-twenty-four, the Cavaticcio Plant takes advantage of a natural drop of fifteen meters.

An impressive turbine, suspended nineteen meters below street level, silently transforms the power of our ancient canals into clean energy, capable of lighting the lives of over twelve hundred Bolognese families. It is the exact same hydraulic force that eight centuries ago powered the mills and silk factories that made Bologna an economic superpower.

Today, protected by the Consorzi dei Canali di Bologna, this plant unites our glorious industrial past with the sustainability of the future... An industrial treasure that continues to flow, alive and unseen, beneath the steps of those walking on the surface."
