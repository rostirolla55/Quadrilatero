Welcome to the intersection of via Tanari Vecchia and via Avesella... Stop for a moment right here, in front of number one via Avesella. Set into this fascinating facade, which closely recalls the Romanesque style, you can notice an elegant niche. Inside, a ceramic tondo with the Madonna and Child, wisely framed by a rich garland of flowers and fruits, still seems to watch over the silence of the alley today. This splendid facade is the work of the famous architects Eduardo Collamarini and Alfonso Rubbiani, historical protagonists of the famous Bolognese medieval revival. If you look up above the arch of the niche, you will notice an inscription... Compagnia dell'Avesella. This name reveals an ancient secret to us. Under your feet, the Avesella canal still flows, invisible and silent. Once upon a time, this area was the beating heart of mills, artisan activities, and laundresses, and the sacred image offered a necessary spiritual protection in a neighborhood that was often subject to sudden flooding. Also notice the small empty niche right on the corner of the wall... Once it perhaps housed lanterns to light up the night, or small protective statues of travelers, removed or unfortunately lost over time. After the Napoleonic era, these historic premises changed life, becoming prestigious ateliers for famous sculptors... Even today, the nearby courtyards preserve intact the timeless charm of the old art workshops. Before continuing your journey, we leave you a little advice. To your right you will find the sanctuary of the rain, another hidden treasure of this incredible neighborhood... Enjoy your continuation among the secrets of Bologna.
