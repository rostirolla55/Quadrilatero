Welcome to the Sanctuary of the Rain... a true secret nestled at the corner of via Galliera and via Riva di Reno. 

Take a moment to admire this grand three-arched portico... it guards one of the most fascinating stories in the city. The roots of this site go back to the thirteenth century, when it was a simple oratory dedicated to Saint Bartholomew. 

However, the name of the rain comes from a fifteen sixty-one miracle... after a procession to end a terrible drought, the sky opened with a life-saving rain that rescued Bologna. 

Let’s step inside the single nave. 

Look for the painting of the Madonna and Child by Michele Di Matteo... it is called invincible because it was found completely intact among the ruins of a building destroyed by fire. Then, notice Agostino Carracci’s Adoration of the Shepherds... the work still bears the marks of a fire caused by bombings during the second world war. 

Admire the powerful terracotta statue by Alfonso Lombardi... and the delicate eighteenth-century frescoes along the staircase. Finally, look for a very rare detail: the original nurse’s robe belonging to Saint Camillus de Lellis... founder of the Camillians. 

This sanctuary is a timeless embrace of faith and art... far from the usual tourist paths. Enjoy your journey through the hidden heart of Bologna.
