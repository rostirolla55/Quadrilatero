"The Legacy of the Carracci between Water and Flesh.

Are you ready to discover the beating heart of Bolognese art? You are in Via Riva di Reno. Beneath your feet flows, silent and hidden, the ancient Reno Canal... which for centuries was the life force of this city.

But look upward... toward the buildings near Piazzetta della Pioggia. Do you see how they are separated? It is the sign of the canal's passage. Right here, under the porticoes where today you see the Macelleria della Pioggia, beat the heart of a family that changed the history of art: the Carracci.

The father of Ludovico Carracci was a beccaro, a butcher. In this neighborhood, between the scent of tobacco from the nearby factory and the sound of the water, Ludovico, Agostino, and Annibale founded... in fifteen-eighty-two... the Academy of the Incamminati.

Enough with the artificiality of Mannerism! The Carracci wanted the natural. Annibale went so far as to paint a butcher's shop with the same respect owed to a sacred work, elevating daily life into great art.

As you walk on, remember that this street is not just asphalt: it is the place where modern realism took its very first steps. Enjoy your walk through history."
