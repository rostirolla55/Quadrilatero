Welcome to Via del Porto 11.

Stop for a moment... and look at the building before you.

At first glance, it appears to be a modern building, perfectly integrated into Bologna's historic walls, but it hides a revolutionary secret.

What you are looking at is one of the most advanced examples of sustainable architecture in the city, the work of architect Aldo Vandini.

Beneath its contemporary façade beats a heart of solid wood, created with ICS-Lam technology. Imagine layers of coniferous wood crisscrossed and glued together under enormous pressure. The result? A material as solid as concrete, yet incredibly light and earthquake-proof.

Vandini wanted to create a dialogue between materials. Inside, the natural warmth of wood; outside, a structure that respects Bolognese proportions while looking to the future of the planet. It is a building that breathes, capable of maintaining the ideal temperature without waste.

As you continue your journey, think of this building as a challenge that has been overcome... proof that innovation can flourish even in the heart of history.
