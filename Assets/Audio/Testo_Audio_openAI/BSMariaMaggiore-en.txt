"The Ancient Heart of Via Galliera.

Welcome to the Basilica of Santa Maria Maggiore... Did you know that you are standing in front of the oldest church in Bologna dedicated to the Virgin Mary? Its roots date back to the fifth or sixth century, built directly over the ancient cardo maximus of the Roman city. Look at the facade: the three-arched portico welcomes you, but imagine that the upper part was only completed in nineteen-fifty-five!

Let us now step inside...

Let your eyes rise toward the vault. That open-sky effect populated by angels is a perspective masterpiece... created between nineteen-thirty-six and nineteen-thirty-eight by Eliseo Fumagalli. Although the basilica was severely damaged by the earthquake of twenty-twelve, today it shines once again thanks to its reopening in twenty-nineteen.

Do not miss the Crucifix of Fico, a wooden artwork dating back to before the year one thousand, and look for the ancient stone cross found during the excavations: it is the symbol of the remote sacredness of this place. Before leaving, admire the refined stucco work by Angelo Gabriello Piò in the Chapel of the Most Holy Sacrament."
