Today we find ourselves in a hidden and fascinating corner of Bologna, where the medieval past dialogues with the city's ancient water soul.

We are in the area of the ancient pugliole. This term, once widespread in various districts of the city, indicated lands that were once meadows or small farms.

In this specific quadrant, enclosed between Borgo Polese, Via del Porto, and the Riva di Reno, the pugliole were closely linked to the life of the canal and the nearby river port.

Just think that right here, in the thirteenth century, stood the first church of Santa Maria delle Pugliole, before the friars moved to fund the great complex of San Francesco.

Walking along these streets, between Via San Carlo and Via Azzogardino, the flow of the roads follows the ancient passages that ran alongside the conduits and the Poggiale stronghold.

A fragment of Bolognese history, which guards the secret of our identity, one step at a time.
