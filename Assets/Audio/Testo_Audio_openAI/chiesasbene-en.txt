Saint Benedict... the church that turned its back on history.

Along the elegant via Indipendenza... there is a church that hides a unique architectural secret. It is not just a place of worship... but an incredible witness to how Bologna has changed over time. A journey eight hundred years long.

Its roots go back to the distant tenth century... when it was a small parish outside the walls. In sixteen hundred and six... it was completely rebuilt by architect Giovanni Battista Ballerini for the Paolotti... the Minim friars... taking on that Baroque and Mannerist face that we still admire inside today.

The incredible inversion of eighteen ninety-two.

The most fascinating curiosity concerns its orientation. For centuries the church faced the noble via Galliera... but in eighteen ninety-two... with the opening of the modern via Indipendenza... the unthinkable happened. In order not to turn its back on the city's new "living room"... the church was literally turned around.

What was the apse was transformed into the new facade... with the addition of the portico we see today. The ancient seventeenth-century facade remained hidden on via Galliera... where it is still visible next to the bell tower.

A treasure chest of Baroque art.

Once you cross the threshold... you forget the noise of shopping to immerse yourself in the silence of Bolognese Baroque. Here are kept the masterpieces of masters such as Giacomo Cavedoni... Alessandro Tiarini... and Lucio Massari... protagonists of that artistic season that made Bologna one of the capitals of European art.

In short... Saint Benedict is not just a church... it is a living lesson in urban planning. A building that knew how to change perspective to remain a protagonist of city life.
