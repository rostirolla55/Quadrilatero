WELCOME TO VIA DEL PORTO 11. STOP FOR A MOMENT AND LOOK AT THE BUILDING IN FRONT OF YOU. AT FIRST GLANCE, IT LOOKS LIKE A MODERN BUILDING, PERFECTLY INTEGRATED INTO THE HISTORIC WALLS OF BOLOGNA, BUT IT HIDES A REVOLUTIONARY SECRET. WHAT YOU ARE LOOKING AT IS ONE OF THE MOST ADVANCED EXAMPLES OF SUSTAINABLE ARCHITECTURE IN THE CITY, THE WORK OF ARCHITECT ALDO VANDINI. BENEATH ITS CONTEMPORARY FACADE BEATS A HEART OF SOLID WOOD MADE WITH CROSS-LAMINATED TIMBER TECHNOLOGY. IMAGINE LAYERS OF CONIFEROUS WOOD CROSSED AND GLUED TOGETHER UNDER ENORMOUS PRESSURE. THE RESULT, A MATERIAL AS SOLID AS CONCRETE, BUT INCREDIBLY LIGHT AND SAFE IN THE EVENT OF AN EARTHQUAKE. VANDINI WANTED TO CREATE A MATERIAL DIALOGUE. INSIDE, THE NATURAL WARMTH OF WOOD. OUTSIDE, A STRUCTURE THAT RESPECTS BOLOGNA'S PROPORTIONS WHILE LOOKING TOWARDS THE FUTURE OF THE PLANET. IT IS A BUILDING THAT BREATHES, CAPABLE OF MAINTAINING THE IDEAL TEMPERATURE WITHOUT WASTE. AS YOU RESUME YOUR JOURNEY, THINK OF THIS BUILDING AS A CHALLENGE WON, PROOF THAT INNOVATION CAN FLOURISH EVEN IN THE HEART OF HISTORY.
