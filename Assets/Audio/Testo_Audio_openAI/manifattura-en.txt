From Tobacco Leaves to the Silver Screen
Welcome to the former Tobacco Factory. [PAUSE - 3 seconds]
Take a look at this grand facade at number 72, Via Riva di Reno. It doesn't look like a typical factory, does it? This is a magnificent example of Art Nouveau style, redesigned in 1906 by architect Gaetano De Napoli.
Imagine that, once upon a time, an open canal flowed right where you are standing. [PAUSE - 4 seconds]
Picture the sound of the water and the bustle of hundreds of women. They were known as the "tabacchine," the factory workers. Women were preferred for this job because it was believed their hands were more skilled and delicate at rolling tobacco leaves into cigars. The factory itself was established here in the early 19th century, inside what used to be an ancient convent.
Today, the spirit of this building has transformed. It is now the heart of the Manifattura delle Arti and home to the Cineteca di Bologna, a world-renowned center for film restoration and culture. If you need a break, the nearby factory gardens offer a peaceful green escape.
