"Looking up or observing the details of Bologna's historic buildings, you may come across a detail as discreet as it is fascinating: house numbers engraved on sandstone plaques. These are not simple indicators, but true fragments of urban history impressed into the typical stone of our hills.

An Identity Material. Sandstone, with its warm coloration fading from gray to gold, is the stone that gave shape to most of Bologna's porticoes and palaces. Using it for house numbering was not just a functional choice, but a way to perfectly integrate public signage with the surrounding architectural fabric, creating a unique visual harmony.

The Evolution of Numbering. These elements bear witness to the crucial transition toward the modernization of the city. Between the late eighteenth and nineteenth centuries, the need to reorganize urban control and postal services led to the introduction of regular numbering systems. The sandstone plaques were carved by hand by skilled artisans, making each piece, with its elegant numerals, a unique and unrepeatable work of art.

Imagine... every single engraving tells of stonecutters who worked the stone in the workshops of the Quadrilatero or the historic center, leaving a mark destined to defy the centuries and guide the steps of generations of citizens and visitors.

A Heritage to Safeguard. Today, many of these ancient plaques have been replaced by modern signs, but those that withstand time represent a small open-air treasure. We invite you to walk slowly, to look beyond the surface, and to discover the elegance of these details that continue to tell the authentic character of our community."
