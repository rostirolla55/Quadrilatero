Coffi and the Adoration of the Shepherds, the masterpiece that sparked debate. 

Let's immerse ourselves in the first chapel on the left and discover the work of Agostino Carracci, painted around 1595. 

Agostino, one of the founders of the innovative Accademia degli Incamminati, presents us here with his vision of the Adoration of the Shepherds.

But what makes this painting so special? ... Its raw, unfiltered realism. 

While today we celebrate it for its modernity, at the time this detail was its condemnation. 

Historical sources reveal that its pronounced naturalism provoked fierce criticism, being judged as "excessive" for the standards of the era.

Carracci dared to show reality exactly as it was... 

And this included, according to his detractors, details that were too real or "vulgar," such as calloused feet in such a work. 

This is Carracci's breaking point...

A piece that chooses visceral authenticity over idealization, marking a moment of profound artistic innovation.

Historical note:... unfortunately, this masterpiece was almost lost. 

It was damaged by a fire during the bombings of World War II.
But fortunately, it is still here to bear witness to the power of its realism.
