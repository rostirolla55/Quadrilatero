The terracotta giant.

Alfonso Lombardi's Saint Bartholomew.

If you enter the Sanctuary of the Madonna della Pioggia, your eyes will immediately be drawn to the high altar.

But there's a protagonist, evicted from history, awaiting you on the upper floor.

It's Alfonso Lombardi's Saint Bartholomew, a monumental statue that, for centuries, has been the beating heart and very symbol of this church.

Imagine the scene: it's 1729... A sudden collapse severely damaged the structure.

During the reconstruction of 1732, Bologna decided to give pride of place to the miraculous cult of the Madonna della Pioggia.

So, the poor Saint Bartholomew, who until then reigned supreme on the high altar, had to cede his throne and was moved to the oratory, where he remains to this day.

But don't be fooled by the move: his power remains intact.
We're talking about a masterpiece from the early 16th century. Alfonso Lombardi, one of the greatest sculptors active in Bologna, sculpted it in polychrome terracotta.

He's a giant, standing 2.25 meters tall, with an imposing physical presence.

Look at the sculpture: it's muscular, tormented, dramatic... Since the saint was martyred by flaying, Lombardi infused the clay with a raw and truthful expressiveness that leaves you breathless.

It's the strength of the earth becoming flesh and spirit.

Finding this statue in the intimacy of the oratory is an almost magical experience: it's a face-to-face encounter with the Bolognese Renaissance.

Next time you visit the Sanctuary, don't stop at the nave; go up the stairs from Via di Reno 124 and look for San Bartolomeo.

You will discover that, even far from the high altar, this terracotta giant continues to tell its story of devotion and beauty for almost 500 years.
