Historical and Architectural Clarifications

To ensure scientific and educational accuracy, two critical elements regarding the property at Via San Carlo, 19 must be highlighted:

  • Chronological Discrepancy: Some commercial listings or tourist descriptions incorrectly state Ludovico's stay as 1555–1609. The correct biographical timeline documents his birth in Bologna in 1555 and his death in 1619.

  • Structural Evolution: The current building features an architectural layout resulting from subsequent 18th- or 19th-century renovations. Therefore, today's structure stands on the area or incorporates elements of an earlier 16th-century core where the Carracci family—historically rooted in this city sector between nearby Via Riva di Reno and Piazzetta della Pioggia—owned workshops or residences.

The Historical Headquarters in Via Rolandino

From the perspective of Bolognese monumental and archival documentation, the memory of the family and the official activity of the Accademia degli Incamminati are historically and predominantly associated with another building in the city: the so-called "Casa Berò Gradi" (or Casa Berò known as Carracci's house), located at Via Rolandino, 1, which underwent major philological restorations during the 19th century.

In summary, while the property at Via San Carlo, 19 represents a fundamental testament to the strong original bond between the Carracci family and the ancient canal quarter, its current commercial promotion should be complemented with Ludovico Carracci's accurate biographical data and the scientific context of Bolognese historical criticism.
